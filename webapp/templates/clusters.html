How a score is built

  1. Vectorize. Every posting and your resume become TF-IDF vectors — each keyword weighted by how distinctive it is. Per-company boilerplate (benefits, EEO text) is stripped first so a company can’t inflate all its postings at once.
  2. Cluster. K-means groups the {{ p.corpus_size }} postings into {{ p.n_clusters }} topic cluster{{ 's' if p.n_clusters != 1 }}. Your resume’s affinity to each cluster’s center is precomputed.
  3. Score. A posting’s raw fit is {{ (p.cosine_weight * 100) | round | int }}% × cosine(resume, posting) + {{ (p.cluster_weight * 100) | round | int }}% × affinity(resume, its cluster).
  4. Scale. Raw scores are divided by the day’s best so the top match reads 100 — scores are relative ranks, not absolute percentages. Open any posting’s breakdown to see its exact math.
